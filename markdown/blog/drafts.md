# Limited energy to love

I believe that just like any form of energy, we all have some sort of a maximum supply of love we can give out, that replenishes over time.

However I think that the battery size and replenishment rate is not static.

# Stepping on people's toes

One of the main ways I love is by trying as best I can not to step on toes. Metaphorically, I classify as having toes any living thing that we reasonably suspect to feel any sort of pain.

The way I see it, the world is not currently equipped to eliminate toe-stepping[1]. Our desires grow faster than our resources; we likely love meat too much to ever give it up as food, etc.

However I believe we can start by finding ways to diminish toe stepping. I think this is possible through trading some of the feeding we get from toe-stepping in exchange for the feeding we derive from our effort in lowering our toe-stepping.

Realistically we won’t fully eliminate toe-stepping this way. In some possible future though, I think we can through sufficient technological progress[2].

[1] I don’t think toe stepping should be “fully” eliminated. I can think about two kids (or wven adults) playing a metaphorical toe-stepping game with each other and having a blast.

[2] Technology gets somewhat of a bad wrap nowadays, justifyingly so. However I think technology is just a tool that we use to reach our desires faster. When our desires do not align, then technology creates suffering. If our desires align, there is no reason for any suffering to occur.

# On the mismatch between intention and reception

I find that there are often large mismatches between intention and reception. It's very complex because we all know there is a difference between what we want and what we need.
But as you get to know people better, you understand them better. You understand what they want, what they need, and you can fine-tune the love you give them in the way they want to receive it.
It is impractical to get to know everyone like you get to know the people close to you. The best you can do is make assumptions given the data you have.

# On political differences, losing love for each other & the sacrifices we can no longer make

When looking at western discourse with regards to politics, one can't help but notice the change of tone over the past decades. People with different political opinions increasingly no longer want to hear each other. It is as if we were moving in the direction of being in one big tribe but that experiment failed and we are now back to many tribes.
I think this situation is reconcilable but I think we will need a fundamentally different approach in order to make it happen. I think that we simply were not ready to fit everybody's core values in that big tribe and thought we could move on while sacrificing some of them.
If we ever wish to merge the tribes again, and this time more successfully, we cannot make the same sacrifices.
