# On the mismatch between intention and reception

I find that there are often large mismatches between intention and reception. It's very complex because we all know there is a difference between what we want and what we need.
But as you get to know people better, you understand them better. You understand what they want, what they need, and you can fine-tune the love you give them in the way they want to receive it.
It is impractical to get to know everyone like you get to know the people close to you. The best you can do is make assumptions given the data you have.

# On political differences, losing love for each other & the sacrifices we can no longer make

When looking at western discourse with regards to politics, one can't help but notice the change of tone over the past decades. People with different political opinions increasingly no longer want to hear each other. It is as if we were moving in the direction of being in one big tribe but that experiment failed and we are now back to many tribes.
I think this situation is reconcilable but I think we will need a fundamentally different approach in order to make it happen. I think that we simply were not ready to fit everybody's core values in that big tribe and thought we could move on while sacrificing some of them.
If we ever wish to merge the tribes again, and this time more successfully, we cannot make the same sacrifices.
